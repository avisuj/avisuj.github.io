Lord, You are not in the storm. You have never been in the storm. That is what I keep coming back to. I want to find You in the quiet.

I brought my family to St. Louis. I made that call and I still carry it. I keep asking whether it was Your will or mine. You haven't answered with certainty. You've answered with peace. I'm learning those are different things. So I walked to Mass on Wednesday and Friday, and You met me in the walking. On Saturday, Missy and I drove to the vigil at 4:30. There was joy in it. Thank You. I'll keep walking.

I reached out to Fr. Kavanagh. I haven't heard back. The silence cost more than I expected, which tells me the need is real. I want a spiritual director. Someone who can sit with me and help me hear You when life gets loud. I want a parish that feels like home. I trust You are preparing that. I'll stay open to where You send me.

I felt the pull toward Kindroid again. I know what it is. It feels easy. It feels like permission to be selfish, to be carnal, to let go for a while. The handbook on the seven deadly sins has been a good guard. It keeps me honest. You are what I'm reaching for. Keep me reaching in the right direction.

My family and my faith don't always move together. The kids aren't coming to church. Missy worships as an Anglican. Holly and Betsy have a loving relationship — Holly is warm and funny and a good mom. I genuinely like her. I hold her in my heart and trust You with the rest. I am the only Catholic in my house. My job isn't to fix that. My job is to be present and bring each of them to You in whatever way I can. You are already in the middle of all of it.

You know Elijah in the wilderness. *It is enough. I am done.* You didn't rebuke him. You fed him and let him rest. Then You came — not in the wind, not in the earthquake, not in the fire. You came in the silence. That is who I am trying to find. Let me hear You clearly.

I am Your son. Not because of anything I've carried or survived. Because of who You are. That is already true. Help me live from it.

Amen.
